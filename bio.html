My name is Jacob Huebner,

and IT is my middle name.

I'm an IT guy.

I’m an IT guy at heart. I’ve known I was going to work with computers since I was little. When I was in high school, I was determined to pursue a degree in IT and IS. Now, I’m doing IT stuff every day. I could talk about my IT job forever. So, I’ll just tell you about my College experience.

I'm an IIT guy.

I’m currently studying at the Illinois Institute of Technology. At IIT, I am studying Information Technology and Management. I have been heavily focusing on topics like project management, leadership, app development, design theory, operations, and security. I love all the opportunities this school has to offer. There’s no shortage of projects to join or lectures to attend. I even had the opportunity to present an app to a mayor. It’s just been fun.

I want to give a special shout out to some of the great people I’ve met here: Juan Bohorquez, Camilo Cortes, Alif Ishmam, Julio Pagan, Daniel Shin, Colin Brennan, Mohammad Salahy, and Karan Patel.

Another shout-out to the fantastic people/professors at IIT: Jeremy Hajek, James and Katherine Papademas, Daniel Krieglstein, James Novak, Kristina Bauer, and Dan Kahn.

I'm an CIT guy.

My college career started in the CIT department at College of DuPage. I explored their IT degree, and eventually got my associates degree in Computer Internetworking Technologies. I gained technical knowledge of IT fundamentals with a focus on building and managing information systems. During my time at College of Dupage, I had the honor of learning from some of the smartest, hardest working, down-to-earth professors in the world. Their guidance helped land me an internship, and ultimately propelled me forward in my IT career.

I want to give a special shout out to some of the professors that I couldn’t be here without: Dr. Felix Davis, Brock Stout, Tony Chen, Steven Havens, and Barbel Thoens-Masghati.
